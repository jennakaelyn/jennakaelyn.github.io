About

Hi there! I'm Jenna-Kaelyn Huang on the right, but just Jenna is fine. On the right is my father's name. I'm a third year computer science student at UC Berkeley and this is my website. To make a long story short, my interests are Reddit, concerts and music festivals, storytelling (read: David Sedaris), interior designing, and last but not least, data structures.

But to keep a long story long, my favorite interest of them all is storytelling. My friends say I'm infamous for my excessively long and verbose stories (sorry Katie) but I like to think there are never enough details to be shared. My favorite author would have to be Davis Sedaris because if you're going to take forever to get to the point, you might as well be funny along the way. My favorite filmmaker is Casey Neistat because I like his narrative style, though that link is actually just a video of him flying a floating shark balloon around NYC. The best subreddit on all of the Reddit would have to be r/AskReddit just because you get the opportunity to hear random stories from people all over the world you'll never get the chance to meet in person. This thread in particular is my favorite one; it really warms my heart.

But what does all of this have to do with CS? Absolutely nothing. If you really wanted to see my technical skills, you'd just look at my resume or ask me how to merge k sorted lists into one large sorted list. Instead, I just thought I'd see if you would stick around for one of my excessively long stories, and it looks like you did. I hope you enjoyed!
